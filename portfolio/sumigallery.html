Japanese Sumi/e Painting and Chinese Brush Painting

Traditional ink painting is often overlooked for its simplicity. I mostly see it reduced to decoration rather than recognized as a fine art. Sumi-e and Chinese brush paintings can be found on chopstick wrappers, restaurant walls, and “Asian-inspired” designs, yet rarely in contemporary art spaces. That disconnect inspired me to explore the form more deeply. In this exploration, I reinterpret techniques practiced since 770 BCE, merging traditional discipline with contemporary expression to reclaim and re-contextualize the medium. Through this process, I’ve come to appreciate the rigor behind work often perceived as simple: each brushstroke demanding precision, patience, and control.
